# **Preamble and Mission Statement** 
Acting as the voice of all graduate and professional students enrolled at Augusta University, the Graduate Student Government Association shall actively work to improve the quality of life for the student body, along with the university and community as a whole. Believing in the right of self-governance, each graduate or professional student enrolled at Augusta University shall be a non-voting member of the Graduate Student Government Association and be entitled to the rights, privileges, and protections thereof.

# **Article I. Name, Purpose, and Structure** 

## **Section I. Name**

1. The name of this organization shall be the Graduate Student Government Association (GSGA) of Augusta University (AU).

## **Section II. Purpose**

1. The GSGA shall facilitate the exchange of information and ideas between the graduate and professional students of all colleges of AU.

2. The GSGA shall serve as a liaison between graduate and professional students, faculty, staff, and the administration to represent student opinions, needs, and interests to the administration; and to disseminate and promote the exchange of information from the administration and faculty to graduate and professional students.

3. The GSGA shall act to increase the quality of life of student services, academic programs, and the AU environment in order to further enhance the education of AU graduate and professional students.

4. The GSGA shall support a variety of social, cultural, intellectual, and recreational events to promote fellowship, personal growth, and involvement of AU graduate and professional students.

## **Section III. Structure**

1. The structure of the GSGA shall be divided into three bodies: the House of Representatives, the Executive Cabinet, and the Judicial Board. The House of Representatives shall consist of a legislature composed of elected graduate or professional students (representing each program) who meet regularly. The Executive Cabinet shall consist of elected students who serve as the chief executive officers of the GSGA. The Judicial Board shall consist of appointed Justices who provide impartial review of GSGA adherence to this Constitution and related governing documents.

   

# **Article II. Executive Cabinet** 

## **Section I. Purpose**

1. The Executive Cabinet is the executive body of the GSGA and has the authority to administer GSGA funds and approve/deny charters and funding requests, as provided in the Bylaws.

## **Section II. Composition**

1. The Executive Cabinet shall consist of the President, Vice President, Secretary, Comptroller, Humanitarian Chair, Public Relations Manager, and Undergraduate Liaison (collectively, the “Executive Officers”).

2. The Executive Cabinet may also include up to five Members-at-Large, appointed by the President as provided in the Bylaws.

3. Executive Officers and Members-at-Large shall be selected from among graduate and professional students of Augusta University who meet the eligibility standards set forth in the Bylaws.

4. Executive Officers shall be elected annually in accordance with procedures established in the Bylaws and shall serve terms of office as defined therein.

5. Vacancies in Executive Cabinet positions, and succession to those positions when an Executive Officer can no longer fulfill their duties, shall be determined pursuant to the procedures set forth in the Bylaws.

## **Section III. Meetings**

1.  The Executive Cabinets shall meet frequently. Explicit meeting requirements are defined in the Bylaws.

## **Section IV. Responsibilities**

1. President

   1. The President shall be the chief executive officer of the GSGA, preside over meetings of the Executive Cabinet and the House of Representatives, and serve as the official representative of the GSGA to the University administration and faculty. The President shall appoint chairs of standing and ad hoc committees as provided in the Bylaws and shall oversee the orientation and transition of incoming Executive Cabinet officers.

2. Vice President

   1. The Vice President shall assist the President in carrying out the executive functions of the GSGA, preside over meetings in the absence of the President, and serve as a resource to standing and ad hoc committees as provided in the Bylaws. The Vice President shall assist with the orientation and transition of incoming Executive Cabinet officers.

3. Secretary

   1. The Secretary shall be the chief records officer of the GSGA and shall be responsible for keeping minutes and attendance records of GSGA and Executive Cabinet meetings, maintaining current contact information for voting members, and ensuring that official records and documents are preserved as provided in the Bylaws.

4. Comptroller

   1. The Comptroller shall be the chief financial officer of the GSGA and shall be responsible for administering the GSGA budget, overseeing the allocation of funds, and reporting on the financial status of the organization in accordance with the procedures established in the Bylaws.

5. Humanitarian

   1. The Humanitarian Chair shall coordinate GSGA service and philanthropy efforts and shall serve as the primary liaison to service-oriented student organizations, as provided in the Bylaws.

6. Public Relations

   1. The Public Relations Manager shall oversee GSGA communications and outreach, including publicity and promotion for GSGA initiatives and events, as provided in the Bylaws.

7. Undergraduate Liaison

   1. The Undergraduate Liaison shall serve as the primary liaison between the Graduate Student Government Association and the Undergraduate Student Government Association and shall facilitate communication and collaboration between the two bodies, as provided in the Bylaws.

## **Section V: Impeachment Procedures**

1. Resignation.  
   Executive Cabinet members may resign at any time by submitting a written letter of resignation to the GSGA President and the appropriate advisor, or, in the case of the President, to the Vice President, the House of Representatives, and the appropriate advisor.  

2. Grounds for Removal.  
   Executive Cabinet members may be removed from office for failure to fulfill their constitutional or bylaw-defined duties, misconduct, or other just cause.  

3. Authority and Vote Required.  
   Upon initiation in accordance with procedures established in the Bylaws, an Executive Cabinet member may be removed from office by an affirmative vote of a simple majority of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established.  
   
4. Due Process.  
   No Executive Cabinet member shall be removed from office without an opportunity to be informed of the allegations against them and to present their position in accordance with the procedures set forth in the Bylaws.

# **Article III. House of Representatives**

## **Section I. Purpose**

1. The House of Representatives of the GSGA is the legislative body for the GSGA.

2. The House of Representatives shall represent all graduate and professional programs and shall hold the power to vote on legislation, resolutions, and Executive Cabinet appointments, as provided in this Constitution and the Bylaws.

## **Section II. Composition**

1. The House of Representatives shall be composed of Representatives from each graduate and professional degree program at Augusta University.

2. Representation in the House shall be allocated among programs in proportion to student enrollment, as provided in the Bylaws.

3. Representatives shall be selected from among graduate and professional students who meet eligibility standards established in the Bylaws.

## **Section III. Elections**

1. Each graduate or professional program shall determine the manner in which its GSGA Representative(s) are selected, subject to minimum standards established in the Bylaws.

2. Representatives shall serve terms of office as defined in the Bylaws.

## **Section III. Meetings and Voting**

1. The House of Representatives shall meet regularly during the academic year, as provided in the Bylaws.

2. A majority of the voting membership of the House shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.

3. Each Representative shall have one vote.

4. Only Representatives in good standing, as defined by the Bylaws, shall be eligible to vote.

5. Except as otherwise provided in this Constitution or the Bylaws, a simple majority of the voting members present at a meeting at which the vote is taken, provided that quorum has been established, shall be required for adoption of all motions, resolutions, bills, or other questions requiring a vote.

6. Resolutions and other items of business may be introduced by any active Representative or member of the Executive Cabinet, as provided in the Bylaws.

## **Section IV. Responsibilities**

1. Representatives shall serve as liaisons between the GSGA and their respective classes, programs, and colleges.

2. Representatives shall be responsible for communicating GSGA information to their constituents and bringing constituent concerns to the GSGA.

3. Additional responsibilities and expectations for Representatives may be defined in the Bylaws.

## **Section V. House Committees**

1. Representatives shall serve as liaisons between the GSGA and their respective classes, programs, and colleges.

2. Representatives shall be responsible for communicating GSGA information to their constituents and bringing constituent concerns to the GSGA.

3. Additional responsibilities and expectations for Representatives may be defined in the Bylaws.

## **Section VI: Resignation and Removal of Representatives**

1. Any Representative may resign at any time by submitting a written letter of resignation to the Executive Cabinet and the appropriate advisor.  
2. Representatives may be removed from office for failure to fulfill their constitutional or bylaw-defined duties, violation of attendance requirements, misconduct, or other just cause.  
3. Upon initiation in accordance with procedures established in the Bylaws, a Representative may be removed from office by an affirmative vote of a simple majority of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established.  
4. No Representative shall be removed from office without an opportunity to be informed of the allegations against them and to present their position in accordance with procedures set forth in the Bylaws.  
5. The Bylaws may provide for automatic removal or loss of voting privileges based on attendance or other objective criteria, consistent with this Constitution.  
   

# **Article IV. Judicial Board** 

## **Section I. Purpose**

1. The Judicial Board of the GSGA shall provide impartial interpretation and oversight of the GSGA Constitution and Bylaws.

2. The Judicial Board shall ensure adherence to governing documents in areas including, but not limited to, funding request procedures, use of parliamentary procedure in meetings, and implementation of constitutional and bylaw changes.

## **Section II. Composition**

1. The Judicial Board shall consist of several Justices, as defined in the Bylaws.

2. Justices shall be selected from among graduate and professional students of Augusta University who meet eligibility standards established in the Bylaws.

3. One Justice shall serve as Chief Justice and shall preside over the Judicial Board and act as a tie-breaker in contentious decisions, as provided in the Bylaws.

## **Section III. Appointment and Term**

1. Justices shall be appointed by the GSGA President and confirmed by a simple majority vote of the House of Representatives.

2. Justices shall serve terms extending until completion of the graduate degree in which they were enrolled at the time of appointment, or until resignation or removal, as provided in the Bylaws.

## **Section IV. Meetings and Responsibilities**

1. The Judicial Board shall meet as necessary to carry out its duties and shall periodically review GSGA operations for adherence to this Constitution and the Bylaws.

2. The Judicial Board shall have such additional responsibilities as may be assigned by this Constitution or the Bylaws, including review of questions of constitutional interpretation and participation in removal proceedings for GSGA officers and Representatives.

## **Section V: Resignation and Removal of Justices**

1. Any Justice may resign at any time by submitting a written letter of resignation to the Executive Cabinet and the appropriate advisor.

2. Justices may be removed from office for failure to fulfill their constitutional or bylaw-defined duties, misconduct, or other just cause.

3. Upon initiation in accordance with procedures established in the Bylaws, a Justice may be removed from office by an affirmative vote of at least three-fourths (3/4) of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established.

4. No Justice shall be removed from office without an opportunity to be informed of the allegations against them and to present their position in accordance with the procedures set forth in the Bylaws.

# **Article V. Bylaws** 

## **Section I. Establishment and Authority**

1. The House of Representatives shall establish a set of rules known as the Bylaws of the Graduate Student Government Association.

2. The Bylaws shall be a lesser authority than this Constitution and shall establish further guidelines and procedures for the operation of the GSGA and its bodies.

## **Section II. Amendment of Bylaws**

1. The House of Representatives shall be governed by the Bylaws, consistent with this Constitution.

2. A two-thirds (2/3) vote of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall be required to amend the Bylaws.

3. The GSGA President may veto any amendment to the Bylaws passed by the House of Representatives. The President may veto a particular amendment only once.

4. A three-fourths (3/4) vote of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall be necessary to override a presidential veto of a Bylaws amendment.

## **Section III. Financial Procedures**

1. Budget development, allocation of funds, and specific funding policies for student travel, student organizations, and journal publications shall be governed by the Bylaws.

# **Article VI. Constitution** 

## **Section I. Ratification and Replacement**

1. Initial Ratification

     This Constitution shall become effective upon:

    1. Approval by at least two-thirds (2/3) of the votes cast by the House of Representatives at a meeting where quorum has been established; and

    2. Approval by the Augusta University Provost

2. Superseding Prior Documents.

   Upon ratification, this Constitution, together with the Bylaws and any associated codes adopted pursuant to it, shall supersede all previous governing documents of the GSGA, which shall be deemed null and void.

3. Comprehensive Revision.

   If the Constitution is deemed in need of major and/or multiple revisions, the GSGA President may call for a Constitutional Review Committee to draft a revised Constitution for the GSGA. A revised Constitution shall replace the existing Constitution and go into effect upon:

    1. Approval by at least three-fourths (3/4) of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established; and

    2. Approval by the Augusta University Provost.

    Upon such approval, the prior Constitution shall be nullified and the newly adopted Constitution shall take effect immediately, subject to any transition provisions contained therein.

## **Section II. Amendments to the Constitution**

1. Form of Amendments.

   Amendments to this Constitution shall be drafted to make deletions, insertions, or other specific changes to its text.

2. Proposal of Amendments.

   Amendments to this Constitution may be proposed by either:

    1. A two-thirds (2/3) vote of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established; or

    2. A petition signed by at least five percent (5%) of the graduate and professional student body, directed to the GSGA President.

3. Constitutional Review Committee.

   1. Proposed amendments shall be reviewed by a Constitutional Review Committee.

   2. The Constitutional Review Committee shall include, at minimum, the GSGA Advisor(s), the GSGA President, and the GSGA Vice President.

   3. The composition, procedures, and timelines for the Constitutional Review Committee may be further defined in the Bylaws.

   4. The Committee shall issue a recommendation to the House of Representatives regarding any proposed amendment.

4. Approval.

   1. Following review by the Constitutional Review Committee, the House of Representatives shall vote on each proposed amendment.

   2. An amendment shall be approved by at least three-fourths (3/4) of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established.

   3. Amendments approved by the House shall be submitted to the Augusta University Provost for final approval.

5. Notice and Effective Date.

   1. After approval by the House of Representatives and the Augusta University Provost, each amendment shall be advertised to the graduate and professional student body for no less than two (2) weeks.

   2. Unless a later effective date is specified in the amendment itself, the amendment shall take effect at the conclusion of the two-week notice period.
