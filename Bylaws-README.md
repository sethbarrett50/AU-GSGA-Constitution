# **Preamble**

These Bylaws of the Graduate Student Government Association (GSGA) of Augusta University are adopted pursuant to the GSGA Constitution. They provide detailed rules and procedures for the governance, operations, and responsibilities of the Executive Cabinet, House of Representatives, Judicial Board, committees, and financial processes of the GSGA. In all cases, these Bylaws are subordinate to, and shall be interpreted consistently with, the GSGA Constitution.

# **Article I. Executive Cabinet** 

## **Section I. Purpose**

1. The Executive Cabinet of the GSGA is the leadership body for the GSGA.

2. The Executive Cabinet shall have the power of granting and approving funding of those student organizations, and funding for student travel.

## **Section II. Composition**

1. The Executive Cabinet shall consist of the following Executive Officers:

   1. President;

   2. Vice President;

   3. Secretary;

   4. Comptroller;

   5. Humanitarian Chair;

   6. Public Relations Manager;

   7. Undergraduate Liaison.

2. The Executive Cabinet may also include up to five (5) Members-at-Large, appointed by the President as needed and confirmed in accordance with these Bylaws.

3. To serve as an Executive Officer or Member-at-Large, a student must:

1. Be a full-time graduate or professional student at Augusta University.

2. Have and maintain a minimum of a 2.5 GPA.

3. Be current full-time student in at least their second semester of their first academic year enrolled in an official AU graduate degree-seeking program, as verified by JagTrax or equivalent official degree progress verification.

4. Be willing to fulfill all responsibility for the position they are elected for.

4. Position-Specific Eligibility and Term Limits:

   1. President.

      1. Must be a current, elected GSGA Representative from an official AU Graduate School program.

      2. Must have prior Student Government Association experience (as an undergraduate or graduate student at Augusta University or another institution) or prior service as a GSGA member.

      3. May serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms as President.

   2. Vice President.

      1. Must be either:

         1. A current, elected GSGA Representative from an official AU Graduate School program; or

         2. A former or current Member-at-Large appointed by a current or former GSGA President.

      2. May serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms as Vice President.

   3. Secretary.

      1. Must be either:

         1. A current, elected GSGA Representative from an official AU Graduate School program; or

         2. A former or current Member-at-Large appointed by a current or former GSGA President.

      2. May serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms as Secretary.

   4. Comptroller.

      1. Must be either:

         1. A current, elected GSGA Representative from an official AU Graduate School program; or

         2. A former or current Member-at-Large appointed by a current or former GSGA President.

      2. May serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms as Comptroller.

   5. Humanitarian Chair.

      1. Must be either:

         1. A current, elected GSGA Representative from an official AU Graduate School program; or

         2. A former or current Member-at-Large appointed by a current or former GSGA President.

      2. May serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms as Humanitarian Chair.

   6. Public Relations Manager.

      1. Must be either:

         1. A current, elected GSGA Representative from an official AU Graduate School program; or

         2. A former or current Member-at-Large appointed by a current or former GSGA President.

      2. May serve no more than two (2) consecutive terms as Public Relations Manager.

   7. Undergraduate Liaison.

      1. Eligibility requirements for the Undergraduate Liaison shall be established in consultation with the Undergraduate SGA and must be consistent with the general eligibility requirements in Section 2.C.

5. Election

   1. Executive Cabinet officers shall be elected and installed in accordance with the procedures set forth in Section III of this Article.

6. Succession.

   1. Succession procedures for Executive Cabinet positions, including the President, shall be as defined in Section III of this Article.

7. Abilities

   1. Executive Cabinet officers are voting members and are allowed to fullfill the usual duties of a representative, including but not limited to presenting resolutions, making motions, etc.

## **Section III. Elections, Appointments, and Succession**

1. Election Notice and Candidate Applications.

   1.  Notice of upcoming Executive Cabinet elections shall be announced at the January GSGA meeting.

   2. The announcement shall include:

      1. The Executive Cabinet positions to be filled;

      2. The dates when candidate applications will open and close; and

      3. Information regarding the Undergraduate Liaison position and its selection process, which shall also be advertised to the Undergraduate SGA.

   3.  Candidate applications for Executive Cabinet positions shall be distributed to the House of Representatives for review at least 1 week prior to election day.

   4. Candidates must meet with the current executive holding the position they are running for prior to the election when possible.

   5. Candidates can run for two (2) positions during an election cycle max. When voting commences, the first position they are elected for is the one they shall hold.

2. Candidate Presentations and Voting.

   1. At the March meeting, each candidate for an Executive Cabinet position shall be allotted the following times for presentation:

      1. President: Six (6) minutes for presenting their platform and four (4) minutes allotted for questions

      2. Other Executive Cabinet Positions: Four (4) minutes for presenting their platform and two (2) minutes allotted for questions

      3. Time will be kept by the Justices

   2. Following presentations, the House of Representatives shall vote to elect Executive Officers in accordance with the voting procedures established in these Bylaws (Article II §V.).

   3. Election results shall be communicated to all candidates and the House of Representatives via email no later than one (1) week after initial elections.

   4. Justices will be tasked with facilitating the elections

   5. Winners will be determined by the candidate that receives the most votes. In cases where three (3) or more candidates run, this may be less than half of the total votes.

3. Unfilled Positions and Mid-Term Vacancies.

   1. If any Executive Cabinet positions remain unfilled following the regular election, or if a vacancy occurs during the term, the President may appoint a candidate to fill the position, subject to approval by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the House of Representatives at a regular meeting.

   2. The appointee must meet all eligibility requirements for the position as outlined in Section 2\.

   3. Upon approval by the House, the appointee shall assume the position and all associated responsibilities for the remainder of the term.

4.  Undergraduate Liaison Appointment.

   1. The Undergraduate Liaison shall be appointed by mutual agreement of the recently sworn-in Undergraduate SGA President and Graduate SGA President.

   2. The appointment shall be presented to the House of Representatives for confirmation no later than the April meeting.

5.  Installation and Start of Term.

   1. All elected Executive Officers shall be sworn in at the first official GSGA meeting following the election.

   2. Executive Officers shall begin their term responsibilities at the April meeting of that academic year.

   3. The Undergraduate Liaison shall be confirmed by the House and installed no later than the April meeting.

6. Succession.

   1.  Succession procedures for Executive Cabinet positions, including the President, shall be as defined elsewhere in these Bylaws. In the event of a vacancy, the procedures in Section III.3 shall apply unless otherwise specified.

## **Section IV. Meetings**

1.  The Executive Cabinet shall meet at least once a month or more as needed.

## **Section V. Responsibilities**

1. President

   1. Responsibilities.

      The GSGA President shall:

      1. Attend all House of Representatives business meetings and meetings of the Executive Cabinet;

      2. Act as the official representative of the GSGA to the University administration and faculty;

      3. Appoint the chairpersons of all standing and ad hoc committees, in accordance with the Constitution and these Bylaws;

      4. Aid in the orientation of new GSGA Representatives;

      5. Provide training and support to the incoming President and assist with the orientation of the incoming Executive Cabinet; and

      6. Assign or delegate additional tasks to Executive Cabinet members as needs arise.

   2. Stipend.

      1. The stipend for the President is two thousand eight hundred dollars ($2,800), paid in four (4) equal installments of seven hundred dollars ($700).

2. Vice President

   1. Responsibilities.

      The GSGA Vice President shall:

      1. Assume the duties and exercise the powers of the GSGA President in the absence or temporary incapacity of the President;

      2. Preside at meetings of the GSGA and the Executive Cabinet when the President is absent;

      3. Serve as an ex officio member of all ad hoc committees and act as a resource person for those committees;

      4. Be responsible for following up with Representatives who are not fulfilling their obligations to attend meetings or participate in assigned committees; and

      5. Provide training and support to the incoming Vice President and assist with the orientation of the incoming Executive Cabinet.

   2. Stipend.

      1. The stipend for the Vice President is two thousand eight hundred dollars ($2,800), paid in four (4) equal installments of seven hundred dollars ($700).

3. Secretary

   1. Responsibilities.

      The GSGA Secretary shall:

      1. Keep an accurate record of the proceedings and attendance at monthly GSGA meetings and Executive Cabinet meetings;

      2. Maintain a current list containing pertinent contact information for each voting member;

      3. Prepare the minutes of GSGA meetings and make them publicly available in a timely manner;

      4. Maintain a file of all valuable documents and records of the GSGA;

      5. Send notices at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance to all GSGA members listing the time, date, place, and agenda of the next meeting; and

      6. Work with the Vice President to follow up with GSGA Representatives who are not attending meetings as required.

   2. Stipend.

      1. The stipend for the Secretary is two thousand one hundred dollars ($2,100), paid in four (4) equal installments of five hundred twenty-five dollars ($525).

4. Comptroller

   1. Responsibilities

      The GSGA Comptroller shall:

      1. Serve as the official financial representative of the GSGA and the GSGA yearly budget;

      2. Serve as chair of the Budget and Allocations Committee;

      3. Coordinate with the Budget and Allocations Committee regarding funding request approvals submitted for student travel and student organizations;

      4. Work closely with the appropriate business specialist to administer GSGA accounts and oversee decisions on allocations;x

      5. Maintain accurate records of all financial transactions made year-to-date and transactions that are still outstanding;

      6. Present updated information on approved or unapproved funding requests, other expenditures, and an up-to-date budget report at each GSGA meeting;

      7. Maintain an accessible public record of the current GSGA budget (e.g., in the designated Box folder); and

      8. At the end of each academic year, follow the proper procedures to prepare and submit the GSGA budget proposal request for the upcoming academic year.

   2. Stipend.

      1. The stipend for the Comptroller is two thousand eight hundred dollars ($2,800), paid in four (4) equal installments of seven hundred dollars ($700).

5. Humanitarian

   1. Responsibilities.

      The GSGA Humanitarian Chair shall:

      1. Serve as a liaison to service-oriented student organizations;

      2. Serve as a standing member of the Budget and Allocations Committee;

      3. Support other Executive Cabinet members as needed; and

      4. Work to foster school-wide community service and philanthropy projects and serve as chair of the Community Service and Philanthropy Committee.

   2. Stipend.

      1. The stipend for the Humanitarian Chair is two thousand one hundred dollars ($2,100), paid in four (4) equal installments of five hundred twenty-five dollars ($525).

6. Public Relations

   1. Responsibilities.

      The GSGA Public Relations Manager shall:

      1. Serve as chair of the Communications Committee;

      2. Manage all GSGA social media accounts and other official communication platforms;

      3. Maintain and post content on all GSGA-affiliated social media accounts at least weekly during the academic year;

      4. Create advertisements and promotional materials for all GSGA-hosted events;

      5. Organize and execute the promotion of all GSGA-hosted events;

      6. Utilize members of the Communications Committee to ensure that promotional materials are effectively distributed; and

      7. Serve as historian, or appoint a Communications Committee member to do so, to document and record photographs and other media at GSGA-hosted events for use in future promotion and organizational reports.

   2. Stipend.

      1. The stipend for the Public Relations Manager is two thousand one hundred dollars ($2,100), paid in four (4) equal installments of five hundred twenty-five dollars ($525).

7. Undergraduate Liaison

   1. Responsibilities.

      The GSGA Undergraduate Liaison shall:

      1. Manage major communication and collaboration between the Undergraduate Student Government Association (USGA) and the Graduate Student Government Association (GSGA) at Augusta University;

      2. Attend at least one (1) GSGA meeting per month and at least one (1) USGA meeting per month;

      3. At each USGA meeting, communicate updates from GSGA Executive Cabinet meetings to which the Liaison is explicitly invited;

      4. At each GSGA Executive Cabinet meeting to which the Liaison is explicitly invited, communicate relevant developments from the USGA; and

      5. Identify and bring forward undergraduate SGA developments that are pertinent for discussion with the GSGA Executive Cabinet.

   2. Term and Compensation.

      1. The Undergraduate Liaison shall serve a term of one (1) academic year.

      2. The Undergraduate Liaison position is unpaid.

   3. Eligibility.

      The GSGA Undergraduate Liaison must be:

      1. A current, elected undergraduate SGA Representative; and

      2. A full-time student enrolled in an official AU undergraduate degree-seeking program.

      Prior experience on Augusta University’s undergraduate SGA Executive Board is preferred but not required.

**Section VI: Impeachment and Removal of Executive Cabinet Members**

1. Resignation.  
   1. An Executive Cabinet member may resign at any time by submitting a written letter of resignation to:  
      1. The GSGA President and the appropriate advisor; or  
      2. In the case of the President, the Vice President, the House of Representatives, and the appropriate advisor.  
   2. Resignations shall become effective upon receipt or on the effective date specified in the letter, whichever is later, unless otherwise agreed by the House of Representatives.  
2. Initiation of Impeachment.  
   1. If there is evidence to suggest that an Executive Cabinet member is not fulfilling their constitutional or bylaw-defined duties, or has otherwise engaged in misconduct, impeachment proceedings may be initiated by a written petition requesting removal.  
   2. The petition must:  
      1. Identify the Executive Cabinet member in question;  
      2. State the specific grounds for removal; and  
      3. Be signed by at least two (2) voting members of the House of Representatives.  
   3. The petition shall be submitted to the Judicial Board and to the GSGA Secretary for recordkeeping.  
3. Hearing Before the Judicial Board.  
   1. Upon receipt of a valid petition, the Judicial Board shall convene a hearing within a reasonable period of time, as defined by these Bylaws or Judicial Board procedures.  
   2. Both the petitioning members and the Executive Cabinet member subject to impeachment shall be given:  
      1. Reasonable notice of the hearing; and  
      2. An opportunity to present their respective cases, including relevant evidence and witnesses.  
   3. Following the hearing, the Judicial Board shall issue a written determination and recommendation to the House of Representatives regarding whether removal is warranted.  
4. House Consideration and Vote.  
   1. After receiving the Judicial Board’s determination and recommendation, the House of Representatives shall consider the question of removal at a regular or special meeting.  
   2. The Executive Cabinet member subject to removal shall remain in good standing and retain all rights and responsibilities of office until the House has taken final action.  
   3. The House shall vote on the question of removal by secret ballot.  
   4. A simple majority of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall determine the result of the vote, unless a higher threshold is required elsewhere in the Constitution or these Bylaws.  
   5. The result of the vote shall be recorded in the minutes, and the Secretary shall notify the Executive Cabinet member and the appropriate advisor(s) of the outcome in writing.  
5. Effect of Removal and Vacancies.  
   1. Upon a successful vote for removal, the Executive Cabinet position shall be declared vacant.  
   2. Vacancies in Executive Cabinet positions shall be filled in accordance with the vacancy and succession procedures set forth in these Bylaws.

# **Article II. House of Representatives**

## **Section I. Composition and Eligibility**

1. Composition

   1. The House of Representatives shall be composed of Representatives from each graduate and professional degree program at Augusta University.

   2. Unless otherwise amended, there shall be one (1) Representative for every fifty (50) students in each program, based on official enrollment figures for that academic year.

2. Eligibility

   To serve as a Representative, a student must:

   1. Be a graduate or professional student at Augusta University;

   2. Maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5; and

   3. Be willing and able to fulfill the responsibilities of the position.

## **Section II. Elections**

1. Method of Election.

   1. The manner of election or selection of each GSGA Representative shall be determined by the program they are representing, provided that the method is:

      1. Transparent to students in the program; and

      2. Consistent with any minimum standards or timelines established by these Bylaws.

   2. Programs shall communicate the names and contact information of their elected Representatives to the GSGA Secretary in a timely manner.

2. Term of Office.

   1. Representative terms of office, including start and end dates, shall be defined by these Bylaws or by GSGA policy adopted by the House.

   2. Vacancies and mid-term replacements shall be filled in accordance with procedures established by these Bylaws.

## **Section III. Meetings**

1. Schedule

   1. Regular meetings of the GSGA House of Representatives shall be held monthly during the first two weeks of each month during the fall and spring semesters, excluding December and May.

   2. The schedule of meetings for the upcoming academic year shall be published in the designated Box folder and emailed to Representatives no later than August 1 of that academic year.

2. Meeting Notice

   1. The agenda for each upcoming meeting, including any attachments, shall be provided to all GSGA Representatives at least forty-eight (48) hours prior to the meeting by email or other electronic medium (e.g., Box, shared drive, or web page).

3. Meeting Agenda and Order of Business.

   1. A meeting may only conduct voting procedures if quorum, as defined in Article II §V. B. a., is reached. If quorum is not reached, the meeting may proceed for discussion and reports, but no votes may be taken, except as provided in subsection 3.3.C.

   2. The routine order of business for regular meetings of the House of Representatives shall be:

      1. Roll Call;

      2. Establishment of Quorum;

      3. Reports from Executive Cabinet;

      4. Reports of Committees;

      5. Old Business;

      6. New Business;

      7. Announcements; and

      8. Adjournment.

   3. If quorum is not met at two (2) consecutive regular meetings, voting procedures may occur at the next meeting on impeachment and membership proceedings, even if quorum is not reached, provided that such procedures are specifically listed on the agenda.

   4. Alternative meeting protocols and orders of business for special-purpose meetings may be adopted by a two-thirds (2/3) affirmative vote of those in attendance.

## **Section IV. Attendance**

1. Attendance Requirements.

1. Representatives are permitted one (1) unexcused absence per semester.

2. Representatives are permitted one (1) additional unexcused absence per academic year.

3. Documented excused absences may include, but are not limited to:

    1. Doctor’s note;

    2. Military service;

    3. Athletic responsibilities;

    4. Educational fieldwork; or

    5. Other circumstances approved by the Executive Cabinet or designated officer.

4. Documented excused absences may *not* include, but are not limited to:

    1. Other extracurricular commitment regardless of if made prior to establishment of GSGA meeting schedule.

    2. Regional campus location as representative and justice has the option to attend online.

    3. Not keeping camera on if attending virtually.

         

2. Submission of Excuses.

   1. Requests for an excused absence, including appropriate documentation, must be submitted at least twenty-four (24) hours in advance of the scheduled meeting, except in cases of emergency.

   2. Documentation and excuse requests shall be submitted to the GSGA Secretary (or their designee) for review.

3. Remote Attendance.

   1. Representatives are permitted to attend meetings online *only* if they are out of town due to educational fieldwork, military service, or other comparable obligations approved by the GSGA Secretary. 

   2. Representatives attending online must have an actively working camera turned on for the duration of the meeting in order to be counted as present.

4. Consequences for Noncompliance.

   1. Failure to comply with these attendance requirements may result in loss of voting privileges or removal from office, as provided in these Bylaws.

   2. Attendance-related automatic removal procedures are further detailed in Section 8 of this Article.

## **Section V. Voting**

1. Eligibility to Vote.

   1. Only GSGA Representatives who are present and in good standing under these Bylaws, including attendance policies, shall be eligible to vote.

   2. Each GSGA Representative shall have one (1) vote.

2. Quorum.

   1. A quorum for regular meetings shall consist of a 50% of the current voting membership of the House of Representatives.

3. Adoption of Motions.

   1. Except as otherwise specified in the Constitution or these Bylaws, a simple majority of the voting members present at a meeting at which the vote is taken, provided that quorum has been established, shall be required for adoption of all motions, resolutions, bills, or other questions requiring a vote.

4. Introduction of Resolutions.

   1. Resolutions or other items of business may be brought forward by any active Representative or Executive Cabinet member.

## **Section VI. Responsibilities of Representatives**

1. Liaison Role.

   1. It is the responsibility of every Representative to serve as the liaison for their respective classes, programs, and colleges.

   2. Any form of communication that the GSGA sends out to be distributed must be emailed or otherwise distributed by Representatives to the rest of the student body they represent.

2. Participation in GSGA Activities.

   1. Each Representative is required to attend at least:

      1. One (1) retreat;

      2. One (1) fundraiser; and

      3. One (1) service project

   per academic year, unless excused under Section 4\.

3. Consequences for Failure to Fulfill Responsibilities.

   1. Failure to fulfill these obligations may result in a loss of voting privileges or other sanctions, as determined by the Executive Cabinet in accordance with these Bylaws.

   2. Repeated or serious failure to fulfill responsibilities may also be grounds for removal under Section 8 of this Article.

## **Section VII. Committees**

1. Standing Committees.

   1. The standing committees of the House of Representatives shall include, at minimum:

      1. Humanitarian Committee; and

      2. Budget and Allocations Committee.

   2. Additional standing committees may be established by the House as needed.

2. Committee Chairpersons.

   1. Chairpersons shall:

      1. Preside over all meetings of their committee;

      2. Ensure that detailed minutes of committee proceedings are recorded, including the date, time, names of attendees, and an accurate summary of discussions and decisions;

      3. Provide committee minutes to the GSGA Secretary or Vice President within one (1) week of each committee meeting;

      4. Report on committee proceedings at monthly meetings of the House of Representatives; and

      5. Prepare a year-end report detailing all events held and decisions made by the committee during the previous year, to be filed with the GSGA Secretary.

   2. The GSGA Executive Cabinet shall appoint chairpersons for each committee at the beginning of each school year and as needed.

3. Committee Membership.

   1. All committee members must be Augusta University graduate or professional students. 

   2. Each GSGA Representative must participate as a member of at least one (1) GSGA committee, including standing, special-purpose, or ad hoc committees during each school year.

   3. Committee members shall:

      1. Attend all committee meetings; and

      2. Be active participants in the committee and assist the committee chairperson as requested.

4. Committee Meetings.

   1. Committees shall meet at least twice per semester (excluding the summer semester) or more often as needed to fulfill their responsibilities.

5. Special Purpose or Ad Hoc Committees.

   1. Special committees may be established by the GSGA President for specific purposes as needed.

   2. The purpose, composition, and duration of each special or ad hoc committee shall be specified by the President with input from the Executive Cabinet and Advisor.

   3. The duration of special or ad hoc committees shall not extend beyond the current academic year unless otherwise specified in the committee charge.

## **Section VIII. Resignation and Impeachment of Representatives**

1. Resignation.

   1. Any GSGA Representative may resign at any time by submitting a written letter of resignation to the Executive Cabinet and the appropriate advisor.

   2. Resignations shall be effective upon receipt or on the date specified in the letter, whichever is later, unless otherwise agreed by the Executive Cabinet.

2. Initiation of Removal (Impeachment).

   1. If there is evidence to suggest that a Representative is not fulfilling their duties or is otherwise in violation of this Constitution or these Bylaws, a petition may be written to remove that Representative.

   2. The petition must:

      1. Identify the Representative in question;

      2. State the specific grounds for removal; and

      3. Be signed by at least three (3) voting members of the House of Representatives.

   3. The petition shall be submitted to the Judicial Board and to the GSGA Secretary for recordkeeping.

3. Hearing and Recommendation.

   1. Both the petitioners and the Representative subject to removal shall have an opportunity to present their respective cases to the Judicial Board.

   2. Following the hearing, the Judicial Board shall issue a determination and recommendation to the House of Representatives regarding whether removal is warranted.

4. House Vote.

   1. The House shall vote on the removal of the Representative by secret ballot at a regular or special meeting.

   2. A simple majority of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall determine the result of the vote.

   3. The Representative shall remain in good standing and retain all rights and responsibilities of office until a final decision is made.

5. Automatic Removal for Attendance Violations.

   1. GSGA Representatives may be removed automatically by the GSGA Vice President, without a removal hearing, if they violate the attendance policy set forth in Section 4 of this Article.

   2. The Vice President shall notify the Representative, the Executive Cabinet, the appropriate advisor, and appropriate class deans or faculty in writing of any automatic removal under this subsection.

   3. A Representative removed under this provision may appeal to the Judicial Board under procedures established elsewhere in these Bylaws.



# **Article III. Judicial Board** 

## **Section I. Composition and Eligibility**

1. Size and Representation.

   1. There shall be one (1) Justice for every two hundred fifty (250) students enrolled at the Augusta University Graduate School, based on official enrollment figures.  
   2. The Judicial Board must include Justices representing at least three (3) distinct graduate programs.  
   3. This representation requirement may limit the GSGA President’s choices when seeking to fill a vacant Justice position.  
2. Eligibility Requirements.  
   To serve as a Justice, a student must:  
   1. Be a current full-time graduate or professional student at Augusta University;  
   2. Be enrolled in at least their second semester of their first academic year in an official AU graduate degree-seeking program, as verified by JagTrax or equivalent official degree progress documentation;  
   3. Maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5; and  
   4. Be willing and able to fulfill all responsibilities of the Justice position.

# 

## **Section II. Appointment and Chief Justice**

1. Appointment.

   1. GSGA Justices are appointed by the current GSGA President for a term extending until they complete the graduate degree in which they were enrolled at the time of appointment, or until resignation or removal under these Bylaws.

   2. Each appointment must be confirmed by a simple majority vote of the voting members of the GSGA House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established.

2. Chief Justice.

   1. The longest-serving GSGA Justice shall serve as Chief Justice.

   2. The Chief Justice shall:

      1. Preside over meetings of the Judicial Board;

      2. Serve as the tie-breaker in contentious decisions of the Judicial Board; and

      3. Oversee the administration of Judicial Board procedures.

   3. If there is no longest-serving Justice (e.g., at the initial formation of the Board or when multiple Justices are appointed at the same time), the current GSGA President shall identify and nominate a Chief Justice from among the Justices upon confirmation of the entire Judicial Board by the House of Representatives.

## **Section III. Meetings**

1. Attendance and General Requirements.

   1. The GSGA Justices shall be subject to the same general meeting attendance requirements as members of the GSGA House of Representatives, as set forth in these Bylaws.

   2. Failure to comply with attendance requirements may result in sanctions or removal as specified in Section 5 of this Article.

2. Required Semester Review Meeting.

   1. The Judicial Board is required to meet with the current Faculty Advisor and the Executive Cabinet once each semester, at the last Executive Cabinet meeting of the semester.  
   2. At this meeting, the Judicial Board shall provide an evaluation of:  
      1. Adherence to the GSGA Constitution and Bylaws in funding request approvals;  
      2. Use of Robert’s Rules of Order or other adopted parliamentary procedure in meetings; and  
      3. Any changes that have been made to the GSGA Constitution or Bylaws in the past semester and their implementation.

## **Section IV. Responsibilities**

1. General Responsibilities.

   The Judicial Board and its Justices shall:

   1. Provide impartial interpretation of the GSGA Constitution and Bylaws when questions or disputes arise;  
   2. Review and evaluate adherence to governing documents in connection with funding requests and meeting procedures;  
   3. Participate in removal and impeachment proceedings for GSGA officers and Representatives as specified in these Bylaws;  
   4. Provide recommendations to the House of Representatives and Executive Cabinet regarding improvements to governance practices and constitutional compliance; and  
   5. Perform any additional duties assigned to the Judicial Board by the Constitution, these Bylaws, or formal action of the House of Representatives.

## **Section V. Resignation and Removal of Justices**

1. Resignation.

   1. Any GSGA Justice may resign at any time by submitting a written letter of resignation to the Executive Cabinet and the appropriate advisor.

   2. Resignations shall be effective upon receipt or on the date specified in the letter, whichever is later, unless otherwise agreed by the Executive Cabinet.

2. Initiation of Removal.

   1. A Justice may be considered for removal if there is evidence of:

      1. Failure to fulfill constitutional or bylaw-defined duties;

      2. Violation of attendance requirements;

      3. Misconduct; or

      4. Other just cause.

   2. Removal proceedings may be initiated by:

      1. An official petition signed by at least five percent (5%) of the graduate and professional student population; or

      2. A petition approved by at least one-third (1/3) of the voting members of the House of Representatives; or

      3. A written petition signed by at least three (3) voting members of the House of Representatives specifically alleging that a Justice is not fulfilling their duties.

   3. Petitions shall be submitted to the GSGA Secretary and the Chief Justice, or, if the Chief Justice is the subject of the petition, to the President of the GSGA.

3. Hearing and Recommendation.

   1. Unless otherwise provided, both the petitioners and the Justice subject to removal shall have an opportunity to present their respective cases before the House of Representatives or a designated hearing panel, in accordance with procedures adopted by the Judicial Board and approved by the House.

   2. The Justice shall have the right to:

      1. Be informed of the specific allegations;

      2. Present evidence and witnesses; and

      3. Respond to evidence presented by others.

   3. Following the hearing, a recommendation regarding removal shall be presented to the House of Representatives.

4. House Vote on Removal.

   1. The House of Representatives shall vote by secret ballot on the question of removal of the Justice at a regular or special meeting.

   2. In accordance with the Constitution, an affirmative vote of at least three-fourths (3/4) of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall be required to remove a Justice from office.

   3. The Justice shall remain in good standing and retain all rights and responsibilities of office until the House has taken final action.

5. Automatic Removal for Attendance Violations.

   1. The Chief Justice shall have the authority to remove any Justice who violates the Judicial Board attendance policy without a formal removal hearing.

   2. The Chief Justice shall notify the Justice, the Executive Cabinet, the House of Representatives, and the appropriate advisor in writing of any automatic removal under this subsection.

   3. A Justice removed under this provision may appeal to the House of Representatives or a designated appellate body under procedures established elsewhere in these Bylaws.

      

# **Article IV. Funding and Finance** 

## **Section I.  Budget Development**

1. Budget Outline.

   1. The annual budget proposal must follow an outline for the upcoming year consisting, at minimum, of:

      1. Officer stipends;

      2. One or more sections for student organization funding;

      3. One or more sections for research and journal publication funding; and

      4. A section for GSGA events and operating expenses.

   2. This outline shall be used as a guide rather than a strict requirement for the final budget.

2. Preparation and Approval.

   1. The budget outline shall be developed by the Budget and Allocations Committee in coordination with the Comptroller and Executive Cabinet.

   2. The budget outline shall be presented to the House of Representatives for approval no later than the second (2nd) regular meeting of the academic year.

   3. The remainder of the proposed budget allocations shall be determined by the Comptroller and Executive Cabinet, consistent with the approved outline and these Bylaws.

## **Section II.  General Funding Procedures**

1. Submission Deadlines.

   1. All funding requests must be submitted prior to the travel or event date.

   2. Funding requests will only be accepted for events or travel occurring in the same academic semester in which the request is submitted.

   3. Exceptions may be made for travel occurring in the first week of a semester; such exceptions shall be decided by the Executive Cabinet in conjunction with the USG Bylaws.

2. Reimbursement Timelines.

   1. The designated business specialist for GSGA accounts retains the right to set reimbursement timelines and documentation requirements in accordance with University policy.

   2. All funding recipients must comply with the timelines and requirements established by the business specialist and the University.

3. Approval Authority.

   1. The Comptroller has the authority to approve “typical” funding requests without separate approval from the Executive Cabinet or Budget and Allocations Committee when:

      1. The request falls within established funding guidelines; and

      2. Sufficient funds remain in the relevant budget line.

   2. “Typical” funding requests and their parameters shall be defined and reviewed periodically by the Budget and Allocations Committee.

   3. New, unique, or exceptional funding requests that fall outside established guidelines must be reviewed and approved by members of the Budget and Allocations Committee by the deadline specified by the Comptroller.

## **Section III. Student Travel Funding**

1. Eligibility.

   1. All graduate and professional students are eligible to request student travel funding to attend or present at a national or international conference, subject to these Bylaws and available funds.

2. Award Amounts.

   1. The amount awarded shall be consistent for all students depending on the type of participation (e.g., attendance only, poster presentation, oral presentation).

   2. Specific award amounts for each participation type shall be determined by the Executive Cabinet prior to the start of each semester and published to the student body.

3. Nature of Requests.

   1. Student travel requests covered by this section are individual and are not part of a student organization funding request.

   2. Group travel requests are not permitted under this funding category.

4. Limits.

   1. Limits or priorities will change on a yearly basis and will be voted on by GSGA representatives at the start of each semester.

## **Section IV.  Student Organization Funding**

1. Eligibility.

   1. Only Augusta University registered student organizations are eligible to receive GSGA funds under this section.

   2. Organizations must remain in good standing with the University and comply with all applicable policies.

2. Funding Limits.

   1. Limits or priorities will change on a yearly basis and will be voted on by GSGA representatives at the start of each semester.

3. Requests and Events.

   1. Registered organizations must submit individual funding requests for each event.

   2. Registered organizations may collaborate with other registered organizations on the same event; however, each organization must submit its own individual funding request for that event.

   3. Registered organizations must have their event registered in Presence. In order for assistance with event registration, organizations must contact Student Life and Engagement.

4. Compliance.

   1. Registered organizations must follow all Georgia Regents and University guidelines for funding requests, purchasing, and reimbursement.

   2. Failure to comply with these guidelines may result in denial of funding, suspension of eligibility, or other sanctions as determined by the Budget and Allocations Committee and Executive Cabinet.

## **Section V.  Review and Revision of Funding Policies**

1. Periodic Review.

   1. The Budget and Allocations Committee shall review these funding and finance policies at least once every two (2) academic years and may recommend changes to the House of Representatives.

2. Amendments.

   1. Any changes to this Article shall be made in accordance with the Bylaws amendment procedures set forth in the Constitution and Bylaws.

# **Article V. Bylaws**

## **Section I. Authority and Scope**

1. Subordination to the Constitution.

   These Bylaws are adopted pursuant to the GSGA Constitution and are subordinate to it. No Bylaws amendment may conflict with the Constitution. In the event of a conflict, the Constitution shall prevail.

2. Governance.

   The House of Representatives shall be governed by these Bylaws, consistent with the Constitution.

## **Section II. Proposal of Bylaws Amendments**

1. Who May Propose.

   Amendments to these Bylaws may be proposed by:

    1. Any voting member of the House of Representatives;

    2. Any member of the Executive Cabinet; or

    3. A standing Rules or Governance Committee, if such a committee is established.

2. Form of Proposals.

   Each proposed Bylaws amendment shall:

    1. Be submitted in writing; and

    2. Clearly indicate the sections to be deleted, inserted, or revised, with proposed new language.

3. Notice.

   1. Proposed Bylaws amendments must be distributed to all voting members of the House of Representatives at least one (1) regular business meeting in advance of the meeting at which the amendment will be considered for a vote.

   2. Distribution may be by email or other official electronic means used by the GSGA.

## **Section III. Review**

1. Optional Committee Review.

   1. The House of Representatives may refer proposed Bylaws amendments to a Rules, Governance, or Bylaws Committee for review and recommendation.

   2. The reviewing committee may suggest edits to ensure consistency with the Constitution and existing Bylaws, but the final text shall be adopted by the House.

## **Section IV. Adoption, Veto, and Override**

1. Adoption Threshold.

   1. In accordance with the Constitution, a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall be required to adopt any amendment to these Bylaws.

2. Presidential Veto.

   1. The GSGA President may veto any Bylaws amendment adopted by the House of Representatives.

   2. The President may exercise this veto only once per amendment and must communicate the veto and rationale in writing to the House of Representatives within a reasonable time frame, not to exceed ten (10) business days from the date of House approval.

3. Override of Veto.

   1. A Bylaws amendment that has been vetoed by the President may be reconsidered by the House of Representatives.

   2. An affirmative vote of at least three-fourths (3/4) of the voting members of the House of Representatives present at a meeting where quorum has been established shall be required to override the presidential veto.

   3. Upon a successful override vote, the Bylaws amendment shall be deemed adopted.

## **Section V. Effective Date and Publication**

1. Effective Date.

   1. Unless a different effective date is specified in the amendment itself, a Bylaws amendment shall take effect immediately upon:

      1. Adoption by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the House of Representatives and either:

         1. Expiration of the presidential veto period without a veto; or

         2. Approval by the President; or

         3. Successful override of a presidential veto under Section 4.3.

2. Publication.

   1. The Secretary shall:

      1. Incorporate all adopted Bylaws amendments into the official text of the Bylaws;

      2. Maintain an up-to-date version of the Bylaws in the designated electronic repository (e.g., Box, shared drive, or web page); and

      3. Notify the House of Representatives and the graduate and professional student body of adopted Bylaws amendments through appropriate communication channels.
